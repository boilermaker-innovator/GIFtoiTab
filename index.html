From GIFs to iTabs: The Complete Journey

The Spark

It was 2019, and CoverCard.com.au was wrapping up. Sitting there with my business co-founder Matt Tomlins, a thought struck me: "How could we have promoted CoverCard better, and for free?"

This led me to the App Store, where I discovered something interesting - a GIF Maker app. As I explored it, I realized how simple it was to select photos, add text, and share to social media. The ease of it hit me hard. Why hadn't I thought of this during CoverCard's active days?

The CoverCard Connection

CoverCard was our solution for simplifying job applications in the mining industry. Based in Perth, I saw firsthand how the Pilbara mining boom created chaos in recruitment. Workers needed multiple certifications, recruitment agents were overwhelmed with paperwork, and the process was crying out for streamlining.

What we built was good - a platform where tradesman could store their qualifications, certifications, and tickets in one place. But perhaps what we were missing wasn't just a better product, but a better way to show it to the world.

The Evolution of an Idea

Living in Adelaide from 2019 to 2024, I kept seeing the same recruitment challenges I'd witnessed with CoverCard. But now, I was thinking about a different angle - not just solving the problem, but how to share solutions effectively.

The name "Gifylink" came from combining the idea of GIFs telling a story with promoting links. At first, people would laugh at the name - GIFs were seen as something funny, not serious. But I saw something deeper: the power of quick, visual information sharing.

The AI Breakthrough

Then ChatGPT arrived, and everything shifted into high gear. Suddenly, brainstorming and prototyping became faster and dynamic. I could explore different approaches, test concepts, and refine ideas rapidly. The Gifylink concept started evolving into something more comprehensive.

The Form Breakthrough

Then came the moment that changed everything - a simple but powerful realization. While exploring ways to create quick summaries of content, I discovered something obvious yet revolutionary: we didn't need AI at all. All we needed was a well-designed form.

This was a game-changer. Instead of complex systems, we could give people simple forms to:

  • Add their key information
  • Upload one relevant image
  • Create professional quick summaries
  • Share their content effectively

The power wasn't in the technology - it was in giving people an easy way to summarize their information. When someone sees an (i) icon, they expect a quick summary of what they need to know. This revelation simplified everything and opened up endless possibilities.

The Power of (i)

This focus on quick summaries led to another crucial realization. While exploring different icons and symbols, the answer was staring right at us - the universal information symbol (i). Everyone already knows what it means, every website user understands it: click for more information.

Why reinvent the wheel when the perfect solution already exists? The (i) symbol is:

  • Universally recognized
  • Professional and trusted
  • Space-efficient
  • Works across all devices
  • Needs no explanation
  • Already part of the web's visual language

Starting Simple

This understanding shaped our starting point. Instead of creating a complex new system, we're beginning with what people already know and trust:

  • A simple (i) icon next to domain names and links
  • Click to see a quick summary
  • One clear image
  • Essential information only
  • Optional action link

It's like a digital business card for your website or product - giving visitors exactly what they need to know in seconds.

WordPress: The Natural First Step

With millions of websites already on WordPress, it became clear where to start. A simple plugin that lets WordPress users add these quick summaries to their:

  • Website previews
  • Product descriptions
  • Service offerings
  • Contact information

Future Growth: Building on Basics

Starting with WordPress is just the beginning. By focusing on simple website and product summaries first, we're building a foundation that can naturally expand:

Phase 1: WordPress Integration
